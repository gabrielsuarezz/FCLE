üìú Founding Documents

Declaration of Independence (1776)

Purpose: Announced separation from Great Britain

Key Idea: "All men are created equal" with unalienable rights to life, liberty, and pursuit of happiness

Author: Thomas Jefferson

Articles of Confederation (1781)

Purpose: First constitution of the United States

Weakness: Created weak central government, no power to tax or regulate trade

Result: Replaced by the Constitution

The Constitution (1787)

Purpose: Supreme law of the United States

Structure: Established three branches of government

Flexibility: Can be amended (27 amendments so far)

The Bill of Rights (1791)

Purpose: First 10 amendments protecting individual liberties

Key Rights: Speech, religion, press, assembly, bear arms, fair trial

Impact: Fundamental protections against government overreach

üéØ Remember: The Constitution begins with "We the People" - emphasizing that government power comes from citizens, not from kings or divine right.

üìú American History Essentials

Colonial Period & Revolution

1620 - Pilgrims Arrive

Mayflower lands at Plymouth Rock. Pilgrims sought religious freedom.

1776 - Declaration of Independence

13 colonies declare independence from Britain. Revolutionary War begins.

1783 - Treaty of Paris

Revolutionary War ends. Britain recognizes American independence.

1787 - Constitutional Convention

Framers write the Constitution in Philadelphia.

19th Century

1803 - Louisiana Purchase

U.S. doubles in size by purchasing land from France.

1861-1865 - Civil War

War between North and South over slavery and states' rights. Union victory preserves nation.

1863 - Emancipation Proclamation

President Lincoln declares slaves in rebelling states to be free.

20th Century Major Events

1917-1918 - World War I

U.S. enters war in 1917. Helps Allied powers defeat Central Powers.

1929 - Great Depression

Stock market crash leads to worst economic crisis in U.S. history.

1941-1945 - World War II

U.S. enters after Pearl Harbor attack. Helps defeat Axis powers.

1950s-1960s - Civil Rights Movement

Movement for racial equality. Led by Martin Luther King Jr. and others.

1991 - Cold War Ends

Soviet Union dissolves. Decades-long tension with U.S. ends.
